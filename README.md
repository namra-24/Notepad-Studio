# 🐰 Cute Notepad

A cozy, kawaii-themed digital notepad — notes, a to-do list, drag-and-drop stickers, a pomodoro timer, five swappable themes (including a terminal easter-egg theme), and a little mascot named Mochi. Pure HTML/CSS/JS, no backend, no build step. All data is saved locally in your browser via `localStorage`.

## Features
- 📝 **Notes** — create, search, pin, format (bold/italic/heading/list), auto-save, download as `.txt`
- ✅ **Tasks** — priorities, progress bar, productivity score, motivational messages
- 🎀 **Stickers** — drag, scroll-to-resize, double-click to remove
- 🍅 **Focus timer** — 25/5 pomodoro cycle
- 🎨 **5 themes** — Light, Dark, Pink, Lavender, Terminal (with a hidden command console)
- 🏆 **Achievements** — unlock badges with confetti as you use the app
- ♿ Reduced-motion support, keyboard-focus states, ARIA labels

## What it's for
Cute Notepad is a personal, browser-based space for the small, everyday things a notebook is usually asked to hold — quick notes, a running task list, a bit of decoration, and a few minutes of focused work. It's built as a single self-contained website, so it lives wherever you put it: a personal portfolio, a private bookmark, or a small corner of the internet just for you.

Everything you create — notes, tasks, stickers, mood entries, theme choice, and unlocked badges — stays saved in your own browser between visits. Nothing is sent anywhere, and nothing needs an account or internet connection to work once the page has loaded.

## Who it's for
- Anyone who wants a lightweight, always-available notepad without signing up for a note-taking app
- People who like a bit of personality in their tools — a mascot, sound, and confetti rather than a blank white page
- Students or freelancers who want notes, a to-do list, and a pomodoro timer in one place instead of three separate apps
- Developers looking for a small, complete example of a themeable, animated, localStorage-backed static site

## How the pieces fit together
The five views — Notes, Tasks, Stickers, Focus, and Settings — share one browser tab and one visual identity, but are meant to be used independently depending on what a visit is for: jotting a thought, working through a task list, decorating a page for fun, running a focus timer, or adjusting how the app looks and sounds. The dashboard widgets (clock, calendar, mood, and quote) sit above all five views as a constant, quiet layer of context regardless of which section is open.

Made with HTML, CSS, and a little JS magic. ✨
